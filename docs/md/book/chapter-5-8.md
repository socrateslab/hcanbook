# 结论
本章探讨了如何对涉及不确定性议题的话语进行**逼真性评估** （ assessing fidelity ）的问题。笔者认为，结合本章论述、此前对融贯性的阐释，以及后续章节对这些原则的具体应用，足以证明叙事理性中存在一套切实可行的逻辑。它超越了既有的各类逻辑体系，为考察修辞能力的核心素养提供了依据——即在充分把握修辞话语潜在价值的前提下，进行创作、运用与评判的能力。笔者承认，在某一专业领域具备修辞能力，需要了解该领域的修辞预设、操作程序与实践惯例，但本文想要强调的是：**通情达理与理性思辨（being reasonable and rational），在任何专业领域、任何社群、任何国家中，都是修辞能力至关重要的组成部分**。这些品质共同构成了叙事理性，在权利平等、决策自由得以保障的环境中尤为重要，各类民主、多元的社会皆是如此。而在专制社群与社会中，尽管“融贯性”的本质与规则、“逼真性”的边界都会受到严格限制，这些品质反而显得更为重要。

### **CONCLUSION**

In this chapter I have dealt with the problem of assessing fidelity in discourse that is about uncertain matters. I believe that this discussion, along with my earlier treatment of coherence and later chapters that will apply these principles, will show that there is a workable logic in narrative rationality. It goes beyond previous logics and provides a basis for considering essential abilities in rhetorical competence—the capacity to make, use, and judge rhetorical discourse with full understanding of its potentialities. I grant that rhetorical competence in a specialized field of endeavor entails awareness of that field’s rhetorical presuppositions, procedures, and practices, but the point I wish to stress is that being reasonable and rational are crucial dimensions of rhetorical competence in any specialty, any community, and any nation. These qualities, constituting narrative rationality, are particularly vital where equality of rights and freedom to decide exist as they do in any democratic, pluralistic setting. They are even more important in autocratic communities and societies, even though the nature and rules of “coherence” and the limits of “fidelity” are circumscribed.
